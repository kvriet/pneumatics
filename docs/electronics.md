## Electronics
Depending on your inflation needs, you might need only one pump, or a variety of pumps, solenoid valves, and pressure sensors. Here are some common configurations you might consider.

### Safety
Many pumps draw 12V, 300mA. This is too much for your Arduino (or other microcontroller) and laptop to power. To solve this, you need an **external power supply**. This can be a 12V adapter or a power bank that can deliver the required voltage. The diagrams below all include an external power supply.

On the inside of the pump are magnets and a coil. When the coil is powered, the magnets start rotating the motor, and the pump starts pumping. When the power is turned off, the magnets will still rotate for a little bit, thus generating current in the coil, which then reverses back into the circuit, and which can harm other components or the power supply. For this reason, you need to add a **flyback diode** to the circuit, so that this excess current can dissipate through the motor itself. The flyback diode has been included in each circuit. Notice the polarity: the negative terminal of the diode is connected to the positive terminal of the pump, and vice versa, because the current generated by the motor comes into the circuit in reverse!
